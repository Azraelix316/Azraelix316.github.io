Math Modeling

For math modeling, I did a lot of work with C++, especially low level C++, to optimize algorithms. I created a library, DSCILIB, currently still a WIP but with many helpful helper functions in order to help make coding more efficient - I learned some more advanced mathematics in linear algebra such as PCA and also transition matrices specifically for this library, which I used to help accelerate my workflow during the HiMCM 2025 competition. It's still a work in progress, but I used many of the matrix based helper functions and QoL, and hopefully it will develop to become a core library. This library was used when I authored a 25-page research paper in 3 days detailing a real-world problem of overtourism in Juneau, Alaska, for the MCM: An international, team-based undergraduate competition to solve a real-world problem utilizing mathematical models. Utilized C++ to create a minimax optimization model accounting for environmental and economic effects of tourism, along with price elasticity models to calculate the exact number of economic incentives and deterrents for optimal tourism. Placed top 30% (Honorable Mention) even at the undergraduate level.
