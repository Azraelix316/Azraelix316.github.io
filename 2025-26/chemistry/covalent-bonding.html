Covalent Bonding

Tags: #chemical-bonding #chemistry
Date: 2025-06-18

  • Contrasted with Ionic Bonding, where you shed electrons, covalent bonding occurs when atoms share orbitals (share electrons) to get a full outer shell
  • The final compound is made up of molecules, not connected ions (like an ionic crystal)
  • Usually formed between nonmetals
  • Can be represented with Lewis Diagrams
  • E.g
  • In the case of Hydrogen, Hydrogen only needs 2 electrons to have a full s shell
  • Special case is Polar Bonding
